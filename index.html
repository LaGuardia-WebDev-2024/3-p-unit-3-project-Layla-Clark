Recipe #1

  • Time: _
  • Serves: _
Ingredients Quantity
Ingredient #1 Quantity #1
Dried Chickpeas (Soaked 6 to 8 hours over night) 250 grams
Water 100-150ml
Table Salt 1 tbsp
Ground Coriander 1 tbsp
Garlic Cloves (pressed) 3
Baking Powder 2 tsp
Fresh Dill (finely chopped) 20g
Plain Flour As needed
Neutral Vegetable Oil (for frying) 1L

Step 1: Drain the water from the chickpeas. Use a food processor or mincer to break down the chickpeas.

Step 2:To use a mincer, place a large bowl under the grinding plate and use the pusher to put the chickpeas through the machine three times. Use a grinding plate with a hole diameter of 6mm or ¼ inch for medium to coarse grinding until the mixture is coarse, thick, and malleable, so that it forms a solid mass when shaped and compressed slightly.

Step 3:To use a food processor, blend the chickpeas with 50ml water for 8 to 10 seconds then pulse to achieve the same texture described above. Transfer the chickpeas to a bowl.

Step 4:After using either method, add the salt, coriander, garlic, baking powder, dill, and 100ml water to the chickpeas then mix well. Ensure the consistency is not too moist or dry: it should easily hold when shaped into a malleable coarse paste. If it's too dry, add more water a splash at a time, and add plain flour in small increments if too wet.

Step 5: Cover a large plate or tray with kitchen towels. Add the oil to a large frying pan on medium to high heat. Make each ta’mia by putting a full tablespoon of the mixture in the centre of one palm. Use the other hand and even it out into a disc. Close the other palm over the mixture to shape the ta’mia in the space in between the palms and apply gentle pressure. Remove the top palm and use the fingers to tidy the edges by pushing them back into the centre. Transfer the ta’mia to the opposite fingers and shape the other side in the same way, ensuring the patty has enough hold to stay intact while frying.
